About Me

I’m James Li, a UW‑Madison student majoring in Economics, Mathematics, and Data Science. I’m passionate about economic systems, financial markets, and monetary policy.

My work spans research at UC Riverside’s Behavioral Lab, leadership in the Federal Reserve Challenge, and advanced coursework in econometrics and international macroeconomics.

Proficient in Python, R, SQL, Tableau, and Stata, I build forecasting models, run regressions, and translate data into policy insights.

Outside of academia, I enjoy coding side projects, exploring IO theory in real markets, and learning about energy economics.
